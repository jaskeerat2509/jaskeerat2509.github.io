Urban Company

+2pp payment success rate ₹8L monthly cost savings

In-House Payment Router

Problem

  • Limited to payment gateways and payment methods the vendor supported — restricting which PGs we could work with and which payment methods we could launch
  • Any vendor outage, however rare, would leave us with no payment processing fallback

Approach

  • Built an in-house dynamic routing layer that monitors payment gateway health in real time and automatically shifts traffic away from underperforming payment gateways
  • Health scoring gives more weight to recent transactions, so the system reacts to payment gateway issues within minutes
  • Traffic splits and all routing configuration are database-driven — changeable instantly with no code deployments

Outcome

  • ~2pp improvement in payment success rate
  • ₹8L/month in direct cost savings by eliminating per-transaction orchestration fees
  • New payment gateway integrations no longer constrained by vendor limitations
  • Resilience improved — a payment gateway outage no longer means zero payment processing capability

Multi-Mandate Retrials — International Markets

Problem

  • Routines (recurring service subscriptions) are 70% of revenue in UAE, Singapore, and KSA — all running on autopayments
  • 20% of routine cancellations were caused by autopayment failures — the system retried the same card four times over four days, then cancelled the routine
  • 25% of customers had multiple saved cards that were never attempted

Approach

  • Built a cascading retrial system: on a card failure, automatically tries the customer’s saved cards in priority order
  • Primary card first, then by most recent use — up to 3 cross-card attempts before escalating to cancellation
  • Retrials only trigger on recoverable failures — not on fraud flags or cases where the customer has explicitly revoked their mandate

Outcome

Weekly payment-related routine cancellations dropped from ~200 to ~70 — a 65% reduction to which this feature was a key contributor. Customers with multiple saved cards now have a meaningful fallback before a subscription is cancelled.

Real Work

Urban Company

63% → 80%+ online payment share ₹45L monthly incremental revenue

Pay Later Convenience Fee

Problem

  • Pay Later bookings had 3× higher cancellation rates than prepaid — costing UC the service partner’s slot and the cancellation incentive with no guaranteed recovery
  • Cash collections carried structural arrear risk: professionals have deduction limits per payout cycle, and churned professionals leave balances unrecoverable

Approach

  • Introduced a convenience fee on Pay Later bookings, shown transparently at the payment option screen
  • Exemption logic designed carefully: prepaid pays nothing; customers auto-falling back to cash due to a payment failure are also exempt — the fee only applies to deliberate Pay Later choices
  • Ran an A/B test before full rollout to measure impact on overall conversion and payment funnel steps — no significant drop observed

Outcome

  • Online payment share moved from 63% to over 80% — fewer Pay Later bookings meant fewer last-minute cancellations, lower arrear exposure, and more predictable earnings for service partners
  • Contributed to ~₹45L in monthly incremental revenue

Real Work

Mastercard

+7pp authorisation rate improvement −2.5pp fraud rate reduction

Acquirer Performance Platform

Problem

  • 200+ acquiring institutions lacked centralised visibility into their payment performance
  • Diagnosing issues across authentication, authorisation, fraud, and chargebacks was manual and fragmented
  • Teams within the same institution operated in silos — one team implementing a fix with no visibility to others

Approach

  • Through internal research, external interviews, and market testing, identified and codified 50+ common problems across auth, fraud, and chargebacks into a structured recommendation framework
  • Each recommendation paired with 4–5 trigger KPIs benchmarked against regional peers; system calculated 200+ KPIs per acquirer in real time
  • Recommendations surfaced only when thresholds were breached — acquirers saw their 10 most impactful actions, prioritised by impact and ease of implementation
  • Built an org-level tracking module so teams within the same institution could see what had already been implemented

Outcome

  • +7pp average authorisation rates and −2.5pp fraud rates across adopting institutions
  • Org-level tracking gave institutions cross-team visibility they previously lacked, reducing duplicated implementation efforts

TELUS Digital

Analytics & Product Case Study

Conversion Funnel Analysis & Landing Page Redesign

Brief: Analyse a 3-day client signup funnel, identify and hypothesise drop-off points, and propose a product solution for the largest drop-off.

Problem identified

  • 18,563 page views → 324 project starts — 1.74% end-to-end conversion
  • Largest drop: 68.5% of visitors never started the intake form — worse for startups (71.3%), healthcare (70.3%), and organic traffic (71%)

Approach

  • Segmented 3-day funnel by traffic source × industry × company size; built 2D conversion heatmaps to surface patterns
  • Formed three hypothesis-driven explanations per drop-off stage
  • Proposed a landing page redesign with wireframes, A/B test plan, and a scroll-triggered chatbot for hesitant users

Targets set

  • Form start rate: 31.5% → 45% within 1 month
  • Bounce rate reduction: 10–15%
  • +5pp lift for startups, healthcare, and organic segments

CARS24

GTM Strategy & Segmentation

Assured Buyback — Go-to-Market Strategy

Brief: Design a detailed strategy for selling Assured Buyback across the CARS24 ecosystem — beyond just car-buying customers.

Problem framed

  • Car owners face resale uncertainty and a slow, fraud-prone selling process
  • Existing buyback programs are brand-locked (Maruti True Value, VW Secure) or offer no guarantee at all
  • CARS24’s network and scale creates room for a brand-agnostic, transparent alternative

Approach

  • Mapped 4 segments (Individual Owners, Fleet Operators, Corporate Buyers, Car Dealers) with distinct pain points and value propositions per segment
  • Designed distribution across digital platform, branch network, and partner channels
  • Built a 4-phase GTM plan with input, output, and health metrics
  • Competitive analysis vs. OEM programs and peer-to-peer alternatives

Differentiator

Brand-agnostic coverage with transparent, upfront pricing and no hidden conditions — directly addressing the key gaps in every existing alternative.
